January Facts

    January 4, 1837 Sarah B. Gibson became the first African American woman to graduate from a U.S. college at Oberlin College in Ohio.
    January 5, 1896 DeHart Hubbard becomes the first African American athlete to compete in the Summer Olympics in Athens, winning a gold medal in the long jump.
    January 7, 1929 Claude McKay published his novel Home to Harlem, exploring the complexities of Black identity in America.
    January 8, 1946 Leontyne Price Becomes First African American Woman to Perform at the Metropolitan Opera
    January 14, 1972 Historian John Hope Franklin became the first African American president of the prestigious American Historical Association
    January 16, 1966- Stokely Carmichael, leader of the Student Nonviolent Coordinating Committee (SNCC), gave his “Black Power” speech in Mississippi.
    January 17, 1942 Joe Louis became the world heavyweight boxing champion after defeating James Braddock. He would go on to hold the title for over 11 years, becoming an American sports icon.
    January 18, 1884 Dr. Daniel Hale Williams performed one of the first successful open-heart surgeries in the United States at Provident Hospital in Chicago, marking a monumental achievement in medicine.
    January 20, 2009 Barack Obama was inaugurated as the 44th president of the United States
    January 21, 1958 Oscar Stanton De Priest, who became the first African American to represent Illinois in the U.S. Congress
    January 22, 1892 Ida B. Wells published a pamphlet titled Southern Horrors: Lynch Law in All Its Phases, which was a detailed investigation into lynching practices and called for federal anti-lynching legislation.
